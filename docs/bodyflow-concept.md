# bodyflow concept

## Core idea

bodyflow is a warm, honest health companion for small, realistic adjustments in training, nutrition, measurements, recovery and mental wellbeing.

If you are genuinely satisfied with yourself and life overall, you may not need this app. If not, bodyflow helps you adjust a little here and there with low-hanging fruit that may increase wellbeing.

## Primary slogan

**A little beats nothing. Every time.**

This slogan is never translated. It remains the same in all languages.

## Supporting philosophy

- **Adjust, don't reinvent.** — Small changes beat total overhauls.
- **Not all at once.** — One thing at a time. Pace yourself.
- **Adjust, don't ignore.** — Face data honestly, respond gently.

## Product stance

bodyflow is NOT:
- A bloatware fitness app with endless features
- A gym-bro app with aggressive messaging
- A medical app that diagnoses or prescribes
- A moralizing coach that shames or judges
- An app about total life reinvention

bodyflow IS:
- Warm and honest
- Practical and direct
- Non-shaming and supportive
- Focused on health gains over perfection

## Core flows

### training-flow
Workouts, sets, reps, weights, supersets, rest timers. The structured training companion.

### nutrition-flow
Calories, food, meals, recipes, daily targets. The food and energy tracker.

### measure-flow
Weight, body measurements (chest, waist, hip), trends. The body data companion.

### mentalflow
How the user feels, given all their data. The emotional check-in.

mentalflow should:
- Account for overall mental state given training load, nutrition, measurements, recovery
- Avoid shame, especially after inactivity
- Support comeback behavior
- Guide the app's tone in responding to the user

### recoveryflow
Rest quality, training load, training frequency, whether food intake matches training load. The readiness assessment.

### batteryflow
A compact, visual recovery/readiness indicator. The quick health snapshot.

## Voice and tone

### Principles
- **Warm:** Human, caring, non-clinical
- **Honest:** Direct about data, not sugar-coating
- **Practical:** Actionable, focused on real solutions
- **Non-shaming:** No guilt, blame, or moral judgment
- **Not happy-go-lucky:** Grounded, not toxic positivity
- **Not motivational fluff:** Real over inspirational
- **Not passive:** Respectfully direct when needed
- **Focused on health gain over perfection:** Progress beats purity

### Good bodyflow messages
- "This is not in line with your stated goal."
- "This is not dramatic."
- "Start with one small thing."
- "Rest is part of training."
- "You do not need to fix everything today."
- "Let's go again?" (comeback message)
- "A little beats nothing. Every time." (comeback slogan)

### Messages to avoid
- "You failed." (too harsh)
- "You ruined your progress." (shame-based)
- "Everything is fine" when the data says otherwise (dishonest)
- "Transform your life." (too ambitious)
- "Become a new person." (reinvention language)
- "You should be ashamed." (shaming)

## Comeback after inactivity

When a user has been inactive for 14+ days and returns:

**Title:** "Skal vi starte igjen nå?"
**Message:** "A little beats nothing. Every time."

This is the moment to be warmest and most encouraging. The user is vulnerable. bodyflow should welcome them back without shame or judgment.

## Feature philosophy

### What bodyflow does well
- Small, manageable logging (one meal, one set, one measurement)
- Honest data reflection (showing trends, not hiding them)
- Realistic expectations (no "transform in 30 days" promises)
- Support for comeback behavior (inactivity detection, warm re-entry)
- Recovery-aware training (not just "do more")
- Mental check-ins tied to data (not standalone mood tracking)

### What bodyflow avoids
- Gamification (no badges, streaks, or competitive elements)
- Extreme transformations (no before/after promises)
- Medical claims (no "cure" language)
- Social comparison (no leaderboards, no feeds)
- Motivational manipulation (no guilt-based reminders)
- Information overload (no bloat, no unnecessary metrics)

## Implementation notes

- The primary slogan "A little beats nothing. Every time." should appear sparingly, in moments of truth: onboarding, comeback, and possibly about/settings.
- Supporting philosophy lines can guide UI copy throughout the app but should not clutter the interface.
- The comeback flow is critical — this is where bodyflow's philosophy is most visible.
- All error messages and empty states should reflect the warm, honest, non-shaming tone.
- Tests should validate that the slogan is never translated and that brand constants exist and match exactly.
